Introduction

Teenagers spend an average of 4.8 hours on social media each day, which means platforms like YouTube and TikTok have a significant impact on their lives. Researchers believe social media has both positive and negative impacts, ranging from the ability to form strong relationships with others to the prevalence of cyberbullying.

It’s important to take a comprehensive look at the effects of social media on students. Large-scale trends can highlight the opportunities and risks that come with using these platforms.

Negative Impacts of Social Media on Students

According to research from Aura, two-thirds of parents say their kids have experienced negative effects from social media. Despite these concerns, many children begin using social media at a very young age.

Mental Health

The U.S. Surgeon General has reported that excessive social media use can increase the risk of anxiety and depression in adolescents. Teens who spend more than three hours per day on social media are more likely to experience poor mental health outcomes.

Academic Performance

Excessive social media use can distract students from schoolwork and reduce concentration. Some students spend so much time online that they neglect homework and other important activities.

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying occurs through social media, messaging apps, and online platforms. Nearly half of teenagers have experienced some form of cyberbullying, which can negatively impact mental health and academic performance.

Body Image and Self-Esteem

Social media can create unrealistic expectations about appearance due to filters and edited images. This can negatively affect students’ self-esteem and body image.

Distorted Perceptions of Reality

Social media can create an unrealistic view of life because people often post only the best moments. This can make students feel left out or not good enough. Researchers link this to FOMO (fear of missing out), which can harm mental and emotional health.

Educators can help by teaching critical thinking so students understand that online content is often edited or selective, and sometimes even misleading.

Positive Impacts of Social Media on Students

Increasing Diversity and Inclusion

Social media can help students connect with others who share similar experiences, even if they live far away. Some teens feel more accepted and supported because they can find communities online.

Enhancing Digital Literacy

Spending time online can help students learn digital literacy skills, such as understanding privacy, recognizing misinformation, and thinking more carefully about what they see and share.

The Role of Educators in Addressing Social Media’s Impact on Students

Teachers can help students build healthy habits by discussing screen time, cyberbullying, and online safety. Educators can also teach digital literacy so students know how to use social media in a safer, more responsible way.

Social media can be a useful tool, but students need guidance from both parents and teachers to reduce risks and build good boundaries.
